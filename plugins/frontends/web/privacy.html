Second Brain

Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 18, 2026

The short version

Second Brain is an art tool. We keep it running by hosting your account, your private archive, and a shared library of skills the agent invents along the way. Everything we collect, why we collect it, and how to get rid of it is listed below.

  • Skills are public and stay with Second Brain. They are code the agent writes; they contain no personal information, and we reuse them to make the platform better for everyone.
  • Shared canvases are public. Anything you push to the gallery can be viewed and remixed by other users.
  • Your saved archive, your email, and your conversations are private to you.
  • Don't share sensitive personal information in chat. This is an art tool. Conversations are technically readable by the operator and may be reviewed for abuse or quality.

Who we are

Second Brain is operated by a solo developer. Questions, takedown requests, and account-deletion problems all go to secondbrainservice@gmail.com.

What we collect

  • Email address — used to send you a magic-link sign-in and a receipt when you pay.
  • Generated images — both the canvases you save to your private archive and the ones you share publicly.
  • Prompts and conversations — the text you send the agent, and the agent's replies.
  • Skills — the small Python programs the agent writes on your behalf to draw, paint, or transform images.
  • Payment confirmation metadata from Stripe (which plan, when, how much). We never see and never store your card details.
  • Basic request logs (IP address, timestamp, browser session ID), kept for abuse prevention and debugging.

What is public vs. private

Public:

  • Anything you share to the gallery — title, image, the name you typed in as the artist.
  • All skills the agent generates. Skills are part of Second Brain's shared library. They survive your account and remain available to other users even after you delete it (the author field is anonymized — see below).

Private:

  • Your saved archive (canvases you click "Save" on).
  • Your email address and payment history.
  • Your conversation transcripts.

How skills work

When you ask the agent to "draw a fractal" or "make me a Mandelbrot," it often writes a new skill — a small Python program — and adds it to the catalog so the next person can use it too. Skills are generated code; they do not contain your email, your prompts, or anything personal about you.

By using Second Brain, you grant us a perpetual, non-exclusive license to host, run, modify, and redistribute any skills the agent produces during your sessions. We do this so the catalog can keep growing for everyone. If a specific skill you caused the agent to make is one you'd like removed, ask the agent to delete it, or email support.

The agent has guardrails against producing explicit, harassing, or prompt-injection-style skills, but these guardrails are not perfect. We remove problem skills when we find them and welcome reports.

Third parties your data touches

  • MiniMax — Second Brain's agent runs on MiniMax's hosted inference platform. Your chat messages and prompts are transmitted to MiniMax for model inference and are subject to MiniMax's privacy policy.
  • Stripe — handles all payments. We never see your card data; Stripe's privacy policy governs that information.
  • Email delivery — sign-in links are sent through a transactional email provider (SMTP). Your email address transits that provider.

Your controls

  • Delete your account. Visit your account page and use the "Delete account" button. This removes your account record, your private archive, your magic-link tokens, your payment history, and any conversations tied to the device you're currently signed in on. Skills and shared-gallery items are kept, but the author field is anonymized.
  • Delete a specific skill. Ask the agent in chat — e.g., "delete the skill called <name>." If a skill won't go quietly, email support.
  • Delete a shared image. Email secondbrainservice@gmail.com with the title and we'll remove it.
  • Get your data. Email support and we'll send you a copy of what's associated with your account.

Conversations from devices you are not currently signed in on may persist after deletion. Email support if you want a full erasure across all your sessions.

Don't share sensitive information

This is an art tool, not a confidant. The operator can technically read conversations (they are stored unencrypted), and may review them for abuse, quality, or to debug issues. The agent can chat about other things and will give general advice if asked, but please don't paste passwords, financial data, medical records, or anything you wouldn't want a stranger to see.

Content rules

We remove and may terminate accounts for: sexually explicit content, illegal content, targeted harassment, attempts to deanonymize other users, and skills that try to exploit or attack the platform. The agent will refuse these on its own most of the time, but the final judgement is the operator's.

Service is provided "as is"

Second Brain is provided as-is, with no warranty. Skills are user-directed generated code. We run them on our infrastructure for you in the demo, and we accept the operational risk of doing so. We do not warrant that generated images, suggestions, or skills are correct, original, safe to redistribute, or fit for any particular purpose, and we are not liable for outcomes that flow from your use of them.

Children

Second Brain is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA / UK). If you believe a child has signed up, email support and we will delete the account.

Jurisdiction and changes

This policy is governed by the laws of the operator's place of residence. We may update it; when we do, we'll change the effective date at the top and surface a notice the next time you sign in. Substantive changes (anything that gives us new rights over your data) will be called out specifically.

Contact

Questions, takedowns, deletion problems, anything else: secondbrainservice@gmail.com.
